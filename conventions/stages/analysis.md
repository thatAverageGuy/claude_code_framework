# Analysis Stage

Analysis synthesizes discovery findings and research data into
actionable insights. Raw information becomes understanding.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## PURPOSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Connect dots across findings and research
- Surface patterns, gaps, opportunities, and risks
- Identify tradeoffs that require decisions
- Prepare ground for strategy — no decisions yet, just clarity

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## WORKFLOW
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

### Step 1: Consolidate
Gather all inputs:
- Discovery findings doc
- All research reports
- User feedback and corrections from prior stages

### Step 2: Cross-Reference
- Do research findings confirm or contradict discovery assumptions?
- Do different research reports conflict with each other?
- Are there patterns across findings?
- What's consistent? What's surprising?

### Step 3: Identify Dimensions
Assess relevant dimensions (use what applies):

**Opportunities**
- Gaps in the market/space
- Advantages this project has
- Favorable timing factors

**Risks**
- What could go wrong (technical, market, operational)
- Assumptions that might be wrong
- External factors that could change

**Tradeoffs**
- Where goals conflict (speed vs quality, cost vs features)
- Hard choices that need to be made
- What's given up with each option

**Constraints**
- What's genuinely fixed vs what feels fixed
- Where flexibility exists
- Real blockers

### Step 4: Synthesize
- Distill into clear picture
- Present to user for discussion
- Identify decisions needed in STRATEGIZE

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## TRANSITION CRITERIA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis is complete when:
- All findings and research are synthesized
- Tradeoffs clearly articulated (not resolved — that's strategy)
- User confirmed analysis is accurate
- Decisions that need to be made are identified
